Early Life and Education

Jeff Kinney was born on February 19, 1971, in Fort Washington, Maryland, and grew up in nearby Potomac. He was the second of four children in his family. Kinney attended Potomac Elementary School and Herbert Hoover Middle School before attending Winston Churchill High School, where he was a cartoonist for the school newspaper.


After graduating from high school, Kinney attended the University of Maryland, where he earned a degree in Computer Science. During his time in college, Kinney created a comic strip called "Igdoof" that was published in the campus newspaper. He also drew cartoons for a local newspaper in College Park, Maryland.







Career as a Game Designer

After college, Kinney moved to Boston and worked as a game designer. He created an online game called "Poptropica" that became very popular, attracting millions of players worldwide. "Poptropica" is a virtual world that allows users to create their own avatars and explore different islands. The game is designed for children aged 6-15 and is intended to be both fun and educational.


Kinney's success as a game designer led him to pursue a career in writing and illustrating. He began working on a book series called "Diary of a Wimpy Kid" that was based on his own experiences as a middle-school student.







The Diary of a Wimpy Kid series

In 1998, Kinney had an idea for a book series called "Diary of a Wimpy Kid" based on his own experiences as a middle-school student. He started writing and drawing cartoons for the book, but was unable to get it published at the time.


In 2004, Kinney started posting "Diary of a Wimpy Kid" on the website Funbrain.com. The book became an instant hit with readers and quickly gained a following. In 2007, the first book in the series, "Diary of a Wimpy Kid," was published in print by Amulet Books. The book became an instant bestseller and was followed by a series of sequels that have sold over 250 million copies worldwide.


The "Diary of a Wimpy Kid" series follows the adventures of Greg Heffley, a middle-school student who is trying to survive the ups and downs of adolescence. The books are written in the form of a diary and include illustrations that help bring the story to life.
